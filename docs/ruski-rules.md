# ruski-rules

A summary of the way Durham ATO plays "Ruski", a variation of the classic beer pong game to play tournaments.

## Overview

Each game will be a 2v2 beer pong game. There are no thirds, no celebrity shots, and no sitting down, no drinking anything that is non alcoholic, no eating, and no use of PEDs (ie nicotine). There will be 10 cups per side and 2 balls in use, meaning each player on a team gets 1 shot every time it is their team's turn to shoot. Cups are arranged pyramid style (4-3-2-1) and are reracked at 6 cups (3-2-1) and at 3 cups (1-1-1).

### Tournament Structure

The tournament will be structured as follows. First, the commissioners determine seeding for the tournament. There are 32 teams in total, which are divided into 8 #1 seeds, #2 seeds, #3 seeds, and #4 seeds. One of each of these is randomly drawn into one of 8 pods, with each pod having 4 teams in total (one of each seed). Pod play requires that all teams in each pod play each other. For each match, the higher rated team will have "playlist rights" over the other, meaning they get to create their own custom playlist to listen to during the game.

### Beginning Play

When beginning play, we enter a Guy2Guy phase to determine who shoots first. Both teams select a shooter who must maintain eye contact with the other team's shooter as they chant "Guy2Guy" and shoot at the same time. If both shooters hit or if both miss, the team's other shooter will be up to do the same thing until one team hits when the other doesn't. That team then shoots first.

### Game Flow

Once Guy2Guy is over, the team that won the Guy2Guy phase shoots first. Whenever a team is officially shooting, either team member can shoot first. On a make, one of the other team's players must drink the cup that was made in 60 seconds, failure to do so will result in the loss of a turn for that shooter. Once a drinking order is set, that order must be maintained for the rest of the game. The only exception is that a player is allowed to "steal" a made cup from their teammate so that they drink it. However, this does not affect the order of drinks, meaning that if a player "steals" a cup then they are next up to drink regardless. This shot flow will be continued until the final cup is hit, during which the game enters the "Redemption" phase. Note that one shot must be shot per song, although this need not be tracket or enforced by the app (currently).

### Redemption

Once a team's last cup is hit, the game enters the "Redemption" phase. This is where even though the last cup was hit, each player from the losing side gets one last chance to shoot a ball. They also get to choose the song they listen to while they shoot. They can keep shooting as many times as they make it. In the event a cup is made, then both teams must drink their cups at the same time (ie losing team drinks last cup, winning team drinks the cup that was just made) under the same 60 second time restriction. In the event that with their redemption shots the losing team hits all of the winning team's cups, the game enters an Overtime phase. This overtime begins with 6 cups arranged normally and follows normal game rules and Redemption rules. This can go on as long as needed with as many overtimes as needed until a team wins.

### Statistics

Statistics tracked are shots made/missed and special shots known as Guys/Dis/Tris. A guy is when a player shoots and not only misses a cup but misses the entire table similar to an airball. After this, the player who Guys must shoot the following shot with their non dominant hand. A tri is a ball that lands in between three cups, and a di is a ball that lands in between two cups. In both cases, all the cups must be drank by the team who got scored on (three in event of a tri, two in event of a di) with one going down each minute and likewise following the same drinking order. Note that Dis and Tris are marked as MISSES, not as makes. A vom is when a player undergoes emesis. In the event of a vom, the player forfeits their next shot.
